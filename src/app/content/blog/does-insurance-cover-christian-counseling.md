It's one of the most common questions I hear: *"Will my insurance actually cover faith-based therapy?"* The short answer is usually **yes — indirectly.** Here's how it works.

## Insurance covers the clinical care, not the faith label

Insurance plans reimburse **mental health treatment for a covered diagnosis**, billed with standard procedure codes (CPT codes) — the same codes used for any psychotherapy. They don't have a separate category for "Christian counseling," and they don't need one.

Because I'm a Licensed Clinical Social Worker, faith-integrated therapy is, clinically and administratively, the same as any other psychotherapy. The faith element is part of *how* we work together; it doesn't change *how the session is billed.*

## What this means for you

- If you have a covered mental health benefit, faith-integrated sessions are typically eligible just like standard therapy.
- More than 30 plans are accepted, including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Humana, Medicare, UnitedHealthcare, and TRICARE.
- Coverage details (copays, deductibles, session limits) vary by plan, so it's worth verifying before you begin.

## Pastoral counseling vs. licensed therapy

One distinction matters here. *Pastoral counseling* offered by clergy is spiritual guidance and generally isn't billable to insurance. *Licensed clinical psychotherapy that integrates faith* — what I provide — is billable, because it's delivered by a licensed clinician and meets clinical standards.

## If insurance isn't an option

If you're uninsured or I'm out-of-network for your plan, there are still paths forward:

- A **sliding scale** is available for those who need it.
- I can provide a **superbill** you can submit for possible out-of-network reimbursement.
- Under the No Surprises Act, you can request a **Good Faith Estimate** of expected costs.
